FIRST AID QUICK REFERENCE

CALL FOR HELP FIRST
Always call emergency services before starting aid. Keep the person calm and still.

CPR (ADULT)
1. Check scene is safe.
2. Tap shoulders. Shout "Are you OK?"
3. No response: call 911 now.
4. Tilt head, lift chin. Check breathing 10 sec.
5. No breathing: give 30 chest compressions. Push hard, fast. Center of chest. 2 inches deep. 100-120 per minute.
6. Give 2 rescue breaths. Pinch nose. Seal mouth. 1 second each. Watch chest rise.
7. Repeat 30:2 until help arrives or person recovers.

CHOKING (ADULT)
1. Ask "Are you choking?" If they cannot speak: act.
2. Give 5 firm back blows between shoulder blades.
3. Give 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich). Stand behind. Fist above navel, below ribs. Pull in and up.
4. Alternate 5+5 until object out or person loses consciousness.
5. If unconscious: begin CPR. Look in mouth before rescue breaths.

SEVERE BLEEDING
1. Press firmly on wound with cloth or bandage.
2. Do not remove cloth if soaked. Add more on top.
3. Raise injured limb above heart if possible.
4. Hold pressure 10-15 minutes without releasing.
5. If limb: tourniquet 2 inches above wound as last resort. Note time applied.
6. Keep person warm and still.

BURNS
Minor (redness, small area):
- Cool under running water 10-20 min.
- Cover with clean non-stick dressing.
- Do not use ice, butter, or toothpaste.

Severe (blistering, large area, face, hands, joints):
- Call 911. Do not remove stuck clothing.
- Cover loosely with clean dry cloth.
- Do not break blisters.

SHOCK
Signs: pale or grey skin, cold clammy, rapid weak pulse, dizziness, confusion.
1. Lay person flat. Raise legs 12 inches (unless head/spine injury suspected).
2. Keep warm with blanket.
3. Do not give food or water.
4. Call 911. Stay with person.

BROKEN BONE
1. Do not straighten.
2. Immobilize joint above and below break with splint (board, rolled clothing).
3. Check pulse and feeling below break every 15 min.
4. Apply ice pack wrapped in cloth to reduce swelling.
5. Seek medical care.

SEIZURE
1. Clear area of hard objects.
2. Do not restrain. Do not put anything in mouth.
3. Turn person on their side when convulsions stop.
4. Time the seizure.
5. Call 911 if: first seizure, lasts over 5 min, injury, not waking up.

POISONING
1. Call Poison Control: 1-800-222-1222 (US).
2. Do not induce vomiting unless directed.
3. Save container or sample if safe to do so.
4. If unconscious: call 911, place in recovery position.

HEAT EXHAUSTION
Signs: heavy sweating, cool pale clammy skin, fast weak pulse, nausea, weakness.
1. Move to cool place. Loosen clothing.
2. Apply cool wet cloths to skin.
3. Sip cool water if conscious.
4. If vomiting or no improvement in 1 hour: call 911.

HEAT STROKE
Signs: hot red dry or damp skin, rapid strong pulse, confusion.
Emergency. Call 911 immediately.
- Cool person rapidly: ice packs to neck, armpits, groin.
- Do not give fluids.

HYPOTHERMIA
Signs: shivering, slurred speech, slow breathing, weak pulse, clumsy, confusion.
1. Move to warm dry place.
2. Remove wet clothing gently.
3. Cover with blankets. Include head. Leave face clear.
4. Give warm beverages (not alcohol) if conscious and able to swallow.
5. Apply warm compresses to neck, armpits, groin. Not hot.
6. Seek medical care.

ALLERGIC REACTION / ANAPHYLAXIS
Severe signs: hives, throat tightening, difficulty breathing, dizziness, rapid pulse.
1. Use epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen) immediately if available.
2. Call 911.
3. Lay person flat. Raise legs.
4. Second EpiPen dose after 5-15 min if symptoms return.
5. Even if symptoms improve: hospital evaluation required.

RECOVERY POSITION
1. Kneel beside unconscious but breathing person.
2. Place near arm at right angle to body, elbow bent, palm up.
3. Bring far arm across chest. Hold back of hand against near cheek.
4. Bend far knee up. Roll person toward you onto their side.
5. Tilt head back slightly to keep airway open.
6. Monitor breathing until help arrives.

END OF FIRST AID REFERENCE
Source: Public domain first aid guidelines.
Always seek professional medical care when available.
